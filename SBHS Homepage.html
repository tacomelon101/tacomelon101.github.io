Clipboard: the new way of managing sport and co-curricular - Updated

Details
Last Updated on Monday, 19 September 2022

From next week the school is starting to introduce a new system for managing sport and co-curricular rolls and schedules. The system is called Clipboard. Mr Barris has been trialling it with Rowing for some weeks, so it's possible you're aware of it.

Initially it will be used for Summer Sport training. Your team and scheduled training sessions will appear in your extra-curricular Clipboard Calendar. Through Term 4, Clipboard will gain Saturday fixtures, then Performance Music ensemble rehearsal and tutoring sessions. From next year all sport and co-curricular activities (think Debating, Chess, Cadets) will be in Clipboard.

So, if you subscribe to the Clipboard Calendar in your preferred calendar system - Google calendar, on your phone etc - along with your Canvas Calendar and you'll have your whole school life set out before your eyes.

Next week Clipboard will appear as My Extracurricular in the Portal Services menu of this portal and will replace My Sport. Right now you can access it directly: portal.clipboard.app/sbhs.

It will also appear in the Parent Portal for your parents to see where you're meant to be after school.

Questions? Use the Feedback and ask away.

What happens about sport absences?
There's no change to the Sport absences procedure. Your parents need to provide an emailed explanation of why you're absent from sport. However, the "I'm going to be absent" button in the My Sport section will no longer be available.

Where can I see my game for Saturday?
This will continue to be on the school website. You will also be able to see it in Clipboard later in Term 4.

I'm in the wrong sport!
Same as before, see Mr Marcos if you're in the wrong sport/want to change sport.

I'm in the wrong team!
Same as before, speak to your coach who will arrange with the MIC of the sport to fix your team. Or you were demoted and just don't know yet.

When can I see my Ren Faire Club planning session schedule in Clipboard?
Ask your teacher. Non-sporting activities will generally not be shown until 2023.

How do I add my Clipboard calendar to my phone?
Easy:

  1. First, go to Clipboard
  2. Make sure your calendar is showing (otherwise click Schedule in the menu)
  3. Tap Actions (top right). A dropdown menu appears
  4. Tap Sync with Calendar App from the menu.

This will open your phone's calendar app and add your extracurricular calendar to your phone.

On your laptop you can right-click Sync with Calendar App and choose Copy Link Address then go into your preferred calendar app, click Subscribe and paste the link you copied.

Why is it called Clipboard?
It replaces teachers/coaches running around tracking information on paper on clipboards. Of course. Yes, the name is Mr Dowdell tier. But is it better than Canvas being a literal and figurative learning Canvas? Comments sought.

Wayne Lee (10E) asks whether your school timetable is available in iCalendar form (in Wayne's case, to add to Google Calendar, but the same applies to whatever calendar you prefer) because if it was, he can then literally organise his whole entire life in calendar form. The answer is: not yet. But the thought is a good one, though not new. Note that, because of the way the Student Portal API works, it's not really possible for your favourite student coder to implement this for you.

Using Vivi in classrooms/libraries

Details
Last Updated on Tuesday, 19 July 2022

Since the end of last year, every learning space at Sydney High is equipped with a Vivi wireless presentation box. You've all seen the big blue splash screen in classrooms when your teachers present something on the projector (unless you're always in rooms with old desktop PCs still available).

Vivi has always allowed you as students to present your screen on the projector too, but it's required you to have the Vivi app and a teacher to invite you. This is OK for when you're in class. But if you're involved in some extra-curricular activity and there's no teacher leading you, you won't have been able to use Vivi.

From this term you can now connect to unused rooms yourself and project your screen. To do this you'll need:

  • To log into the Vivi app, or if you can't install apps, Vivi web app
  • To join to the classroom you're in. This requires a room code which the Vivi will project on the bottom-left of the projector screen.
  • There to be no teacher joined to the room - a teacher will trump your access and they'll then have to invite you to share your screen.

Some information has been published in the Help section of this portal: FAQ - Projectors and Vivi.
